About Content Architecture

Content architecture defines how information is structured and linked across a website, impacting both user experience and search engine visibility.

A strong site architecture improves crawlability, ensuring search engines can efficiently discover and index content.

Silo structures and hub-and-spoke models organize content into logical groups, boosting topical authority and relevance.

Internal linking distributes authority across pages, while preventing orphan pages that cannot be discovered by search engines.

Optimizing page depth, breadcrumb navigation, and crawl hierarchy helps search engines understand the importance of each page.
